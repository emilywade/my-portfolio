MODULES

Game Theory (84%)

In my 4 years of University, this module was by far my favourite! It introduced the mathemcatical study of multiple interactive agent decision making. The module used Python to teach us about Game Theory fundmentals including Nash equilibria and Evolutionary Game Theory. My favourite aspect of the module was the individual coursework, where we had to write a 2 page report on ANY aspect of Game Theory. I chose to research the application of AI in 2 player games, specifically Noughts & Crosses.

Mathematical Methods for Data Mining (68%)

Data mining techniques are widely applicable to problems in industry, science, engineering and government. We covered key aspects of data mining such as clustering, support vector machine analysis and semi-supervised learning, and the most important algorithms including k-means algorithm, k-nearest neighbour classification, PageRank algorithm, Naiäve Bayes. I really enjoyed exploring current techniques within this field and how they could be applied to the real-world in the future.

Stochastic Processes for Finance & Insurance (70%)

Stochastic processes play a key role in analytical finance and insurance and in financial engineering. This course presented the basic models of stochastic processes including Markov chains, Poisson processes and Brownian motion. The module was oriented towards real-life applications of stochastic models, which I found to be extremely interesting and relevant.

Theoretical & Computational Partial Differential Equations (69%)

Differential equations formed a vital part of my degree. This module gave an introduction to the classical analytical treatment of second-order linear partial differential equations and techniques for their numerical solution. We investigated the accuracy and stability of the numerical schemes, and used Python to explore and present findings.
